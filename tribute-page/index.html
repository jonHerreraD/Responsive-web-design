Avenged Sevenfold

Avenged Sevenfold Career Timeline

  • 1999 - Formation

    Avenged Sevenfold was formed in Huntington Beach, California by teenage friends Matt Sanders (M. Shadows), James Sullivan (The Rev), Zachary Baker (Zacky Vengeance), and Matt Wendt. The band's name was inspired by the biblical story of Cain and Abel. What started as a garage band among high school friends would eventually grow into one of the most successful metal acts of the 2000s. At this early stage, they were simply four young musicians experimenting with their sound and learning their craft.

  • 2001 - Debut Album

    The band released their first studio album "Sounding the Seventh Trumpet" when the members were just eighteen years old. Originally released on Good Life Recordings, this debut showcased their early metalcore sound with aggressive screaming vocals and heavy breakdowns. The album was raw and unpolished compared to their later works, but it demonstrated the band's potential. While it didn't achieve commercial success, it served as an important stepping stone and helped them gain a small but dedicated following.

  • 2002 - Lineup Changes

    Brian Haner Jr. (Synyster Gates) joined as lead guitarist, bringing technical prowess and melodic sensibilities that would become integral to their sound. Johnny Christ replaced Matt Wendt on bass, completing what would become the classic Avenged Sevenfold lineup. These lineup changes brought new energy and musical chemistry to the band, with Synyster Gates' guitar skills perfectly complementing Zacky Vengeance's rhythm work. The addition of these members marked the beginning of the band's more serious approach to their music and career.

  • 2003 - Breakthrough with "Waking the Fallen"

    "Waking the Fallen" represented a significant step forward, showcasing improved songwriting, production, and musicianship. The album featured popular tracks like "Unholy Confessions" and "Second Heartbeat," which became fan favorites. While maintaining their metalcore roots, the album demonstrated the band's growing ambition and ability to craft memorable hooks. The production quality was notably better than their debut, attracting attention from larger record labels and a broader fanbase.

  • 2005 - Mainstream Success with "City of Evil"

    "City of Evil" transformed Avenged Sevenfold from underground metalcore act to mainstream metal sensation. The album achieved significant commercial success with singles like "Bat Country," "Beast and the Harlot," and "Seize the Day." M. Shadows completely abandoned screaming vocals in favor of clean singing, alienating some fans but opening doors to mainstream acceptance. The album's blend of heavy metal, hard rock, and progressive elements established them as leaders in the new wave of American heavy metal.

  • 2007 - Self-Titled Album Success

    Their self-titled fourth album debuted at number four on the Billboard 200, becoming their first top-five album. Released through Warner Bros., it featured hit singles "Critical Acclaim," "Almost Easy," and "Afterlife." This was their first self-produced album, demonstrating growing confidence and control over their artistic vision. The success solidified their position as major players in the rock and metal world.

  • 2008 - Major Tours and Recognition

    Following their self-titled album's success, Avenged Sevenfold embarked on extensive touring including headlining major festivals and world tours. The band gained significant radio play and MTV exposure, with their music videos becoming staples on rock television. This period marked their full emergence as mainstream rock stars, performing alongside established acts and drawing increasingly large crowds. Their live performances became known for energy and spectacle, building a devoted fanbase.

  • 2009 - Tragedy Strikes

    On December 28, 2009, The Rev (Jimmy Sullivan) died unexpectedly at age 28. His death was both a personal devastation and professional crisis since he had been one of the band's primary songwriters. The loss threatened to end the band entirely, as The Rev's unique drumming style and creative contributions had been integral to their sound. His death marked the end of an era and left the music world mourning a talented musician who helped define modern metal drumming

  • 2010 - "Nightmare" Era

    Despite their grief, the band created "Nightmare," their fifth studio album and tribute to The Rev. The album debuted at number one on the Billboard 200 and was later certified platinum. Much of the album had been written with The Rev before his death, making it both a celebration of his life and heartbreaking farewell. Mike Portnoy of Dream Theater stepped in as session drummer to complete the album and subsequent tours.

  • 2011 - New Drummer

    Arin Ilejay officially joined as their new permanent drummer, facing the challenging task of replacing The Rev. Ilejay brought his own style while respecting the foundation The Rev had established. The band continued touring extensively to promote "Nightmare," with Ilejay gradually finding his place within the group's dynamic. This period represented the band's effort to move forward while honoring their past and establishing a new identity.

  • 2013 - "Hail to the King"

    "Hail to the King" showcased a more classic heavy metal approach inspired by bands like Metallica and Iron Maiden. The album was certified platinum and debuted at number one on the Billboard 200. This release represented their conscious effort to pay homage to metal heroes while creating something distinctly their own. The album's straightforward, anthemic approach maintained their commercial momentum and proved they could adapt their sound while retaining their core identity.

  • 2015 - Drummer Change

    Arin Ilejay departed the band, leading to another period of transition for their rhythm section. Brooks Wackerman, formerly of Bad Religion, joined as their new drummer, bringing punk and alternative rock experience. His technical skill and versatility allowed the band to explore new directions while maintaining power and precision. Wackerman's addition marked the beginning of a new creative phase for Avenged Sevenfold.

  • 2016 - "The Stage"

    "The Stage" was released as a complete surprise, representing their most ambitious and experimental work as a concept album about artificial intelligence and technology. The album featured their longest and most progressive compositions, incorporating orchestral arrangements and complex musical structures. While receiving critical acclaim for ambition and sophistication, it was their lowest-charting album in years. Nevertheless, it demonstrated their artistic growth and willingness to take creative risks.

  • 2023 - Return with "Life Is But a Dream..."

    After a seven-year gap, they returned with "Life Is But a Dream...," peaking at number 13 on US charts. The album showcased an even more experimental approach, incorporating diverse musical styles and orchestral arrangements. The release featured unusual song structures and collaborations that pushed boundaries of what could be considered an Avenged Sevenfold album. While divisive, it proved their commitment to artistic evolution rather than repeating past successes.

  • 2024-Present - Continued Legacy

    Celebrating 25 years as a band, they continue touring and making festival appearances while maintaining their position as one of metal's most successful acts. Their influence on modern metal is undeniable, having helped bridge the gap between underground metalcore and mainstream metal. With over eight million albums sold in the US alone and multiple platinum certifications, they've achieved both commercial success and critical recognition. Their journey represents one of modern metal's great success stories, marked by artistic evolution, tragedy, and ultimate triumph.
